Getting Started

Build your first autonomous path in mikGen in about five minutes.

Selecting a Format​

To get started, first select the format you will be using. The format determines which segments are available, what they are named, and what constants they use, since mikGen matches your chosen library's internals. It is recommended to read your library's documentation:

  • mikLib
  • LemLib
  • JAR-Template
  • EZ-Template

If your robot is holonomic you can select that here:

important

Swapping formats will reset all your segment constants. See Format Differences for how the formats compare.

Building Your First Path​

To add the start of the path, left click on the field

You can then left click again to add a Drive to Point segment

The most common segments are added with these clicks:

ButtonSegmentDescription
Left ClickpointDriveDrives the robot to a desired (x, y)
Ctrl + Left ClickposeDriveDrives the robot to a desired (x, y, theta)
Right ClickpointTurnPoints the robot at the drive segment in front of it

Every segment and keybind is listed in Segments & Keybinds.

A few tips while building:

  • Drag any waypoint on the field to move it. Hold Ctrl while dragging to snap to the grid.
  • New segments are inserted after the currently selected segment.

Editing a Segment​

Click a segment on the field or in the path list to select it.

Press Backspace to delete a segment and Ctrl+Z to undo.

Playing the Path​

To play your path, press spacebar or click the play button at the bottom of the screen

You can scrub through the path with J and L, and the loop button replays the path continuously.

Copying the Code​

Once your path is ready, press Ctrl+Shift+C to copy the generated code for the whole path, or Ctrl+C to copy only the selected segments. The output uses your chosen library's API, so it can be pasted directly into your autonomous routine:

chassis.set_coordinates(0, 0, 0);
chassis.drive_to_point(24, 24);

mikGen can also write code directly into your project files. See Exporting & Saving.
