Format Differences

mikGen mirrors each library's internals, naming conventions, and constants, so paths simulate and export the way your library actually behaves. For tuning details, read your library's own documentation:

  • mikLib
  • LemLib
  • JAR-Template
  • EZ-Template
important

Swapping formats resets all segment constants to the new format's defaults. Segments the new format does not support are converted to their closest equivalent.

Selecting the Holonomic option (available with mikLib) changes poseDrive to Holonomic to Pose and adds Strafe Distance as its own segment.

Segment Names​

The same segment kind is labeled with your library's naming convention:

SegmentmikLibLemLibJAR-TemplateEZ-Template
pointDriveDrive to PointMove to PointDrive to PointOdom Drive
poseDriveDrive to PoseMove to PoseDrive to PoseOdom Boomerang
distanceDriveDrive to Distance-Drive to DistanceDrive
pointTurnTurn to PointTurn to PointTurn to PointOdom Turn
angleTurnTurn to AngleTurn to HeadingTurn to AngleTurn
angleSwingSwing to AngleSwing to AngleSwing to AngleSwing
pointSwingSwing to PointSwing to Point--

Units​

Distances are always in inches, angles in degrees, and times in milliseconds. The speed scale depends on the library:

FormatMax speed scale
mikLib0 to 12 volts
LemLib0 to 127
JAR-Template0 to 12 volts
EZ-Template0 to 127

Constants​

Each format exposes its own library's constants and exit conditions, so the same idea goes by different names: mikLib uses settle_error and settle_time, LemLib uses smallError and largeError with separate timeouts, and EZ-Template uses small_exit_time and big_exit_time. mikGen uses your library's defaults, and every constant can be edited per segment from the path menu. Refer to your library's documentation for what each constant does and how to tune it.
